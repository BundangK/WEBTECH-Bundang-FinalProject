Skillet Pork Chops and Beans

Savor the hearty flavors of our Skillet Pork Chops and Beans! Juicy, seasoned pork chops are seared to perfection and paired with a savory mix of tender beans, creating a satisfying meal that’s both comforting and nutritious. This one-pan dish is not only easy to prepare but also packed with protein, making it perfect for a busy weeknight. The blend of spices and the rich juices from the pork infuse the beans with delicious flavor. Whether for family dinners or casual gatherings, this recipe is sure to impress!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup seasoned croutons (from a 5-oz. package)
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 boneless, center-cut pork chops (each 6 to 7 oz. and 1 inch thick)
  • 1 ¼ teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 1 cup finely chopped yellow onion (from 1 onion)
  • 2 medium carrots, halved lengthwise and thinly sliced crosswise (about 1 cup)
  • 1 tablespoon thinly sliced fresh sage
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic (from 3 cloves)
  • 2 cans (15.5 oz. each) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup lower-sodium chicken stock

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Place croutons in a zip-top bag and crush with a rolling pin until coarse crumbs form.
  2. Heat oil in a 12-inch oven-safe skillet over medium-high. Pat pork chops dry and season with ½ teaspoon salt. Add pork to skillet; cook, undisturbed, flipping once, until lightly browned on both sides, about 3 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate.
  3. Add onion and carrots to the skillet. Cook over medium, stirring often, until the onion softens and starts to brown, about 6 minutes. Add sage and garlic; cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Stir in beans, stock, and remaining ¾ teaspoon salt.
  4. Nestle pork chops in the bean mixture. Bake until a thermometer inserted in the thickest portion of pork chops registers 145°F, 8 to 12 minutes. Sprinkle crouton crumbs around the pork chops.
